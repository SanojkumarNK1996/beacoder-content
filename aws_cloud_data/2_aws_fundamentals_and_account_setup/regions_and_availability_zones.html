🌍 AWS Regions and Availability Zones

📍 Regions

A Region is a separate geographic location. Each region is fully independent (separate infrastructure, billing, etc.). Examples: us-east-1 (N. Virginia), eu-west-1 (Ireland), ap-south-1 (Mumbai).

⚡ How to Choose a Region

  • Compliance: Data must stay in a specific country
  • Latency: Pick region closest to your users
  • Cost: Prices vary by region (us-east-1 is often cheapest)
  • Services: New services launch in us-east-1 first

🏢 Availability Zones (AZs)

Each region has multiple AZs (3–6). AZs are physically separate but connected by low-latency fiber. Deploying across AZs = high availability.

Rule: Design for Multi-AZ. If one AZ goes down, your app keeps running. RDS Multi-AZ, ALB, and Auto Scaling all span AZs automatically.

🎯 Summary

Regions for global distribution, AZs for fault tolerance within a region. Multi-AZ deployment is non-negotiable for production.
