Embedding Media

🎯 Why This Exists (Indian Real-Life Example)

  • YouTube videos, Spotify audio players, and Google Maps on Indian business sites are all embedded media.
  • Embedding media keeps users on your site while showing content from other platforms.

📖 What Is This

  • Embedding media means displaying audio, video, maps, or other content inside your page.
  • HTML provides <audio>, <video>, and <iframe> for this.

🧾 Syntax / Basic Usage ⭐

<audio controls>
  <source src="song.mp3" type="audio/mpeg" />
  Your browser does not support the audio element.
</audio>

<video controls width="320" height="240">
  <source src="movie.mp4" type="video/mp4" />
  Your browser does not support the video tag.
</video>

<iframe
  src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/VIDEO_ID"
  width="560" height="315" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen
></iframe>

🔍 How It Works (Step-by-Step Execution) ⭐

  1. <audio> and <video> load media files from your server or CDN.
  2. <iframe> embeds another web page (like a YouTube player or map) inside your page.

When to Use

  • Product demo videos, background music, podcasts, tutorials, maps.

When NOT to Use

  • Don’t autoplay audio with sound—it annoys users.
  • Don’t embed untrusted external content for security reasons.

Minimum You MUST Remember

  • Use controls attribute to show play/pause UI.
  • Use allowfullscreen for video iframes when needed.

Common Mistakes

  • Not providing fallback text for unsupported browsers.
  • Incorrect aspect ratios causing black bars or distortion.
