⚡ Divide and Conquer

🎯 What is Divide and Conquer?

Divide and Conquer solves a problem by:

  1. Divide: Split the problem into smaller subproblems
  2. Conquer: Solve subproblems recursively
  3. Combine: Merge the solutions

📋 Classic Examples

  • Merge Sort: Divide array in half, sort, merge
  • Quick Sort: Partition around pivot, recurse
  • Binary Search: Discard half the search space
  • Matrix Multiplication (Strassen): Reduces from O(n³) to O(n^2.81)
  • Closest Pair of Points: O(n log n) geometric algorithm

📐 Master Theorem

Analyzes D&C recurrences: T(n) = aT(n/b) + f(n)

  • Merge Sort: T(n) = 2T(n/2) + O(n) → O(n log n)
  • Binary Search: T(n) = T(n/2) + O(1) → O(log n)

🎯 Summary

Divide and Conquer is the conceptual framework behind the most elegant algorithms. Many O(n log n) algorithms are D&C at heart.
