🟢 Introduction to Greedy Algorithms

🎯 What is a Greedy Algorithm?

A greedy algorithm makes the locally optimal choice at each step, hoping it leads to a globally optimal solution.

📋 When Does Greedy Work?

Greedy works when the problem has:

  1. Greedy choice property: A locally optimal choice can be part of the global optimum
  2. Optimal substructure: The problem's optimal solution contains optimal solutions to subproblems

⚠️ Greedy vs DP

  • Greedy: Makes one irreversible decision per step — faster but doesn't always work
  • DP: Explores all options, guaranteed correct — but slower

🎯 Summary

Greedy is elegant and fast when it applies. The key challenge is proving the greedy choice is safe — through exchange argument or cut-and-paste proof.
